Primary bone cancer remains uncommon, accounting for 0.2% of all malignancies and 5% of childhood malignancies. In the United States, an estimated 3,600 new cases of PBC will be diagnosed in 2020, with 1,720 deaths, making up 0.3% of all cancer deaths. The National Cancer Institute data shows in adults, chondrosarcoma (40%) is most prevalent, followed by osteosarcoma (28%). In children and adolescents, osteosarcoma (56%) is most common, with Ewing sarcoma (34%) second. Chordoma, undifferentiated pleomorphic sarcoma, adamantinoma, fibrosarcoma, and giant cell tumor of the bone are also types of PBC; however, they are fewer in number.
