The differential diagnosis for HFMD should include conditions that present with maculopapular or vesicular rashes with or without oral lesions.

- Erythema multiforme

- Herpangina

- Herpes simplex

- Herpes zoster

- Kawasaki disease

- Toxic epidermal necrolysis(TEN)

- Viral pharyngitis

- Rocky Mountain spotted fever

- Varicella zoster infection (chickenpox)

- Steven-Johnson syndrome

- Monkeypox - In the context of an ongoing outbreak, it becomes important to consider the difficulty in clinically differentiating between monkeypox and HFMD
