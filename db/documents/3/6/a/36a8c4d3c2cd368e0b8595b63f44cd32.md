The following etiologies of jugular foramen syndrome have been described in the literature

- Congenital: 

- Primary cholesteatoma

- Vascular: 

- Asymmetry and enlargement of the jugular foramen
- High or protruding jugular bulb
- IJV thrombosis
- Aneurysms of the extracranial internal carotid artery or vertebral artery
- Jugular diverticulum

- Inflammatory:

- Giant cell arteritis
- Wegener granulomatosis

- Infectious:

- Varicella-Zoster virus (VZV) infection
- Herpes virus infection
- Parapharyngeal abscess

- Neoplastic:

- Benign: paraganglioma,
- Malignant: metastasis,

- Traumatic:

- Penetrating trauma
- Fractures involving the posterior skull base
