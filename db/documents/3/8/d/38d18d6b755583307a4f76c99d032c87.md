Cushing disease is an endocrine disorder characterized by excessive adrenocorticotropic hormone (ACTH) production by the anterior pituitary, leading to the release of an excess of cortisol from the adrenal glands. This activity explains the evaluation and treatment of Cushing disease as well as the interprofessional team's role in managing patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Summarize the etiology of Cushing disease.
- Describe the actions of ACTH in the pathophysiology of Cushing disease.
- Review the physical exam findings associated with Cushing disease.
- Outline some interprofessional team strategies that can improve increase collaborative care and improve patient outcomes.
