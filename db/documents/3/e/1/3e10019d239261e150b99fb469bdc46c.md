Human trafficking is a prevalent and global problem in Texas. In 2019, Texas had the second-highest reported cases of human trafficking, according to the National Human Trafficking Hotline. (4) This is likely because Texas is a large, diverse state with a long international border. Texas law requires healthcare providers to be educated about human trafficking. Texas law also establishes processes for monitoring, ending, and preventing human trafficking. This activity discusses trafficking in general and in Texas. This activity will help all healthcare providers to identify and intervene in trafficking cases.

**Objectives:**
- Identify common signs of human trafficking.
- Summarize the components of the legal definition of human trafficking by the Action-Means-Purpose model and identify important exceptions.
- Describe physical exam findings associated with human trafficking.
- Summarize the importance of trauma-informed care and motivational interviewing in human trafficking victims.
