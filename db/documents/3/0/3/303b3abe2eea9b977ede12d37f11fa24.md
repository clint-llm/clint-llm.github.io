Psychiatric symptoms are a common comorbid feature of hypothalamic hamartoma with epilepsy. They are most disabling and are a significant challenge for the patient and their families. Most commonly, psychiatric symptoms present with externalizing behaviors such as aggression and rage attacks. This activity reviews the evaluation, treatment, and management of psychiatric symptoms and discusses the role of the interprofessional team in the care of patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the etiology of hypothalamic hamartoma with epilepsy.
- Outline the typical presentation of psychiatric symptoms in patients with hypothalamic hamartoma and epilepsy.
- Describe the treatment and management of psychiatric symptoms in patients with hypothalamic hamartoma and epilepsy.
- Explain the importance of improving care coordination, collaboration, and communication amongst an interprofessional team members to enhance care for patients with psychiatric symptoms associated with hypothalamic hamartoma and epilepsy.
