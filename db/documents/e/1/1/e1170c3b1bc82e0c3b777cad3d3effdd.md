Potential complications for a temporoparietal fascia flap may include

- Alopecia

- Flap necrosis

- Frontalis and corrugator supercilii muscle weakness following an injury to the frontal branch of the facial nerve

- Venous congestion

- Wound breakdown

- Hematoma formation

- Infection

- Unfavorable scarring

- Paresthesia

Most complications can be avoided with meticulous dissection and careful flap elevation that respects the local scalp anatomy. Rates of flap loss (partial vs. total) are roughly 2.44%.
