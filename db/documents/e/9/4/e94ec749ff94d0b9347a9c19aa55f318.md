Lung cancer is the most common cancer and cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide.

The global distribution for lung cancer incidence in 2018 was: Asia (58.5%), Europe (22.4%), North America (12.1%), LAC (4.3%), Africa (1.9%), and Oceania (0.81%).

Approximately 85% of all lung cancers are NSCLCs. Adenocarcinoma and squamous cell carcinoma are the most common subtypes, accounting for 50% and 30% of NSCLC cases, respectively.
