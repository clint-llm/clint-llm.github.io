The lifetime prevalence of obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is 1.6% to 2.3% in the community; point prevalence is 1%.

About 50% of those with OCD have the onset of symptoms in childhood and adolescence.  It is unusual to have OCD initially present over the age of 40.

The average time-to-treatment is 11 years.

Roughly 90% of those with OCD have coexisting psychiatric diagnoses, most commonly are anxiety disorders.

Males present earlier, but in adulthood, more females are affected. Postpartum females are up to two times as likely as the general female population to develop OCD.
