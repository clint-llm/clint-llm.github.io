Gastric resection remains the preferred surgical treatment option for many benign and malignant diseases of the stomach.

Despite a steady decline in the incidence and mortality of gastric carcinoma observed over the last century, the absolute number of cases increases annually due to an aging population.

Gastric cancers can be divided into the intestinal type and diffuse type. The most common underlying etiology of intestinal-type carcinoma is Helicobacter pylori infection, which can develop into atrophic gastritis with intestinal metaplasia.H. pylori infection, gastric ulcers, gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD), tobacco or alcohol use, diet, and chemical exposure.

This article will discuss the relevant anatomy, indications, contraindications, procedure details, and complications associated with gastric resection.
