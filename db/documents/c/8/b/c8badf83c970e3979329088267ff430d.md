Autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease is a disorder in which there is an enlargement of the kidneys with multiple cyst formation. There is a decline in renal function and the cysts gradually enlarge as the disease progresses. This activity outlines the evaluation and treatment of polycystic kidney disease and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in managing patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the pathophysiology of polycystic kidney disease.
- Identify the extrarenal manifestations in the history and physical examination of polycystic kidney disease.
- Explain the ultrasonographic criteria used in the evaluation of polycystic kidney disease.
- Review the importance of collaboration and communication among the interprofessional team to enhance delivery of care, including blood pressure control and regular renal function monitoring in patients affected by polycystic kidney disease.
