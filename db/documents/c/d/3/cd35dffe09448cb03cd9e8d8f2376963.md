Chronic pain is a leading chief complaint in an adult ambulatory care setting. Unfortunately, chronic non-cancer pain is often poorly understood by practioners as it does not respond to pharmacological monotherapy. This activity reviews the evaluation, management, and treatment of chronic non-cancer pain with highlighting the role of the interprofessional team in the care of patients with chronic non-cancer pain.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the pathophysiology of chronic pain
- Recognize the key components in the history and physical exam in the evaluation of a patient with chronic non-cancer pain
- Summarize the treatment options for chronic non-cancer pain
- Outline the importance of an interprofessional team approach in treating patients with chronic non-cancer pain
