Serum sickness is a rare but important cause of fever, arthritis, and rash in both children and adults. This activity reviews the differences between the pathophysiology, clinical presentation, diagnosis and management of serum sickness versus serum-sickness-like-reaction. This activity highlights the role of the interprofessional team in caring for patients affected by serum sickness.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the pathophysiology of serum sickness and compare it to that of serum sickness-like-reaction.
- Review the clinical presentation of serum sickness and serum sickness-like-reaction.
- Describe the diagnostic evaluation of a patient with serum sickness.
- Explain interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication in the management of serum sickness.
