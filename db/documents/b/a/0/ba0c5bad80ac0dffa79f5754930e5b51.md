**Oral**

Ranitidine is available as tablets, capsules, or oral syrup. Ranitidine solution or the dissolved tablet may be mixed with select enteral tube feeding solutions.

**Intramuscular (IM)**

Administer as an undiluted solution.

**Intravenous (****IV)**

Intermittent IV bolus: Dilute with a compatible IV solution prior to use to a maximum concentration of 2.5 mg/mL. Then the solution may be administered at a maximum rate of 10 mg per minute.

Intermittent IV infusion: Dilute with a compatible IV solution prior to use to a maximum concentration of 0.5 mg/mL. Then the solution may be administered at a maximum rate of 2.5 to 3.5 mg per minute.

Continuous IV infusion: Dilute 150 mg ranitidine in 250 mL of a compatible IV solution. This solution then may be administered at a rate of 6.25 mg per hour. For patients with Zollinger-Ellison disease, a faster infusion rate may be required.  Dilute prior to use to a maximum concentration of 2.5 mg/mL. Then the infusion may be started at a rate of 1 mg/kg per hour to a maximum dose of 2.5 mg/kg per hour. Ranitidine may be added to total parenteral nutrition. However, its stability varies greatly depending on the specific ingredients, concentrations, and packaging of the final product.
