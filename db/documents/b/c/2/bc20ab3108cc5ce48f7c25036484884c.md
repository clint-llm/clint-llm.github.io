The digital nerve block is a simple procedure that can be performed to provide immediate anesthesia for a multitude of injuries and procedures, such as fractures, dislocations, laceration repair, fingernail removal, or drainage of infections. It is one of the most commonly performed nerve blocks performed in the emergency department due to its wide variety of uses and efficacy. This activity reviews the technique of a digital nerve block, its indications, contraindications, and potential complications.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the indications of a digital nerve block.
- Outline the technique of performing a digital nerve block.
- Summarize the potential complications of a digital nerve block.
- Review the importance of improving care coordination among interprofessional team members for patients undergoing a digital nerve block.
