Medullary carcinoma is a rare and distinct subgroup of breast carcinomas. Despite its highly malignant characteristics, medullary breast carcinoma carries a favorable prognosis compared with the more common infiltrating ductal breast carcinoma. This activity describes the evaluation and treatment of medullary breast carcinoma and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in evaluating and treating patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology of medullary breast carcinoma.
- Describe the appropriate evaluation of medullary breast carcinoma.
- Summarize the treatment and management options available for medullary breast carcinoma.
- Outline interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance the diagnosis of medullary breast carcinoma and improve outcomes.
