According to the Toxic Exposure Surveillance System, there were 3165 human exposures to cyanide from 1993 to 2002. Of that number, only 2.5% were fatal. Fire is the most common source of cyanide exposure in industrialized countries such as the United States. Approximately 35% of all fire victims will have toxic levels of cyanide in their blood on presentation for medical treatment.
