COX inhibitors are medications used in the management and treatment of chronic pain syndromes. They are in the anti-inflammatory class of drugs. This activity reviews the indications, action, and contraindications for COX inhibitors as valuable agents in the management of a variety of different pain syndromes in many various diseases. This activity will highlight the mechanism of action, adverse event profile, and other key factors (e.g., off-label uses, dosing, pharmacodynamics, pharmacokinetics, monitoring, relevant interactions) pertinent for members of a multi-disciplinary health team in the management of patients with chronic pain syndromes and related conditions.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the mechanism of action of COX inhibitors.
- Describe the adverse effects and contraindications of COX inhibitors.
- Review the toxicity of COX inhibitors.
- Summarize interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance COX inhibitor use and improve outcomes.
