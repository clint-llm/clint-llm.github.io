Obesity surgery is growing in popularity throughout the world. While patient selection is a key factor in determining the success of the surgery, post-operative care is equally as important in order to identify and treat complications and optimize long-term health and nutrition status. This activity reviews types of bariatrics surgeries and describes commons post-operative complications and their management. The role of the health care team is imperative throughout all stages of care in bariatric surgery patients.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the following types of bariatric surgery and the associated mechanism of weight loss: Adjustable gastric band, laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy, roux-en-y gastric bypass, duodenal switch, and/or biliopancreatic diversion. 
- Identify postoperative surgical, medical, and nutrition complications of the various bariatric surgeries
- Outline the benefits of bariatric surgery and discuss which surgeries are associated with the best outcomes in terms of weight loss and comorbidity resolution. 
- Review the importance of collaboration and communication among surgeons, internists, dieticians, and psychologists to improve outcomes for patients receiving bariatric surgery.
