Complications after a stroke include worsening neurological status from the extension of the stroke or hemorrhagic conversion and a multitude of other complications associated with prolonged immobility and hospitalization. According to one multicenter study the incidence of complications after a stroke in the acute phase were:

- Recurrent stroke - 9%

- Epileptic seizure - 3%

- Urinary tract infection - 24%

- Pneumonia - 22%

- Pressure sores - 21%

- Deep venous thrombosis - 2%

- Pulmonary embolism - 1%

- Depression - 16%

- Anxiety - 14%
