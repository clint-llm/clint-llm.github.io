Staphylococcus saprophyticus is a Gram-positive bacterium that is a common cause of uncomplicated urinary tract infections, especially in young sexually active females. It is also responsible for complications including acute pyelonephritis, epididymitis, prostatitis, and urethritis. This activity illustrates the evaluation and management of Staphylococcus saprophyticus infections and describes the role of the interprofessional team in managing those with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the infections caused by S. saprophyticus.
- Review the pathophysiology of S. saprophyticus infections.
- Summarize the treatment options for S saprophyticus.
- Outline the importance of improving care coordination among the interprofessional team in enhancing the delivery of care for those with this condition.
