There are no restrictions on physical activity for patients with nephrotic syndrome, and staying active is preferred over bed rest as it reduces the risk of blood clots. Adverse effects of steroids, such as slowing growth, can be detected by monitoring patients every three months in the outpatient clinic. Patients should be given information that bone health is essential, and due to steroids, their bone health can be affected; therefore, supplemental calcium and vitamin D may be protective.
