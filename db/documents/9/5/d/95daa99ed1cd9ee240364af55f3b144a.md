Intracranial subdural empyema is a loculated collection of pus in the subdural space between the dura mater and the arachnoid. It is a serious condition and can lead to significant morbidity and mortality. To avoid the high morbidity and mortality associated with this condition, it must be promptly diagnosed and treated. This activity reviews the evaluation and treatment of subdural empyema and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in evaluating and treating patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology of subdural empyema.
- Outline the appropriate evaluation of patients with subdural empyema.
- Review the management options available for subdural empyema.
- Describe interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance the care of subdural empyema and improve outcomes.
