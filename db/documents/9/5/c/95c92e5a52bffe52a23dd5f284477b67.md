Complications of frontal sinus fractures are typically divided into two categories based on chronicity: acute (less than 6 weeks) or chronic (greater than 6 weeks), but complications overlapping these timeframes may occur. Comlications include but are not limited to:

- Frontal sinusitis

- Meningitis

- Cerebrospinal fluid leak

- Mucocele

- Mucopyocele

- Osteomyelitis

- Pneumocephalus

- Poor aesthetic outcome

- Brain abscess

- Chronic frontal headaches

- Extrusion of graft material

- Intracranial hemorrhage

- Diplopia

- Ophthalmoplegia

- Blindness

- Paresthesia of the supraorbital, infraorbital, and/or supratrochlear nerves

- Hypoesthesia or paresthesia of the ophthalmic nerve (V1) or maxillary nerves (V2)

- Facial deformity
