The use of marijuana, especially during pregnancy, is on the rise. This is due to increasing legal availability and perceived safety regarding its use during pregnancy compared to other drugs. Extensive counseling should ideally start before conception and be performed at every possible patient contact. In addition, women should be screened for drug use and referred when necessary to rehabilitation centers. Exposure to THC during pregnancy places the mother and infant at risk of adverse outcomes. However, there is a lack of strong evidence, and most of the current evidence is derived from cohort or case-control studies.
