Inherited C3 Deficiency

Inherited C3 deficiency occurs because of a secretory malfunction (as a result of a protein substitution).

Paroxysmal Nocturnal Hemoglobinuria

Deficiencies in molecules that inhibit complement (MCP, decay-accelerating factor (DAF), CD59, and Factor H) results in hemolysis or paroxysmal nocturnal hemoglobinuria. Clinical signs of hemolysis include hemoglobinuria at night, anemia, and increased abdominal pain.

Hereditary Angioedema

Hereditary angioedema is an autosomal dominant disorder due to a C1 inhibitor protein deficiency.
