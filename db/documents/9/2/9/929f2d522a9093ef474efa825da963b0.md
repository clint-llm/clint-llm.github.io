Sinus node dysfunction is a disease that is characterized by the inability of the sinoatrial node of producing an adequate heart rate that meets the physiologic needs of an individual. This activity reviews the etiology, diagnostic approach, and management of sinus node dysfunction and highlights the role of interprofessional team members in collaborating to provide well-coordinated care and enhance patient outcomes.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology and epidemiology of sinus node dysfunction.
- Describe the evaluation of sinus node dysfunction.
- Outline the treatment and management options available for sinus node dysfunction.
- Summarize interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance sinus node dysfunction and improve outcomes.
