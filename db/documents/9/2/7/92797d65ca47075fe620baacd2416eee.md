For X-Linked Agammaglobulinemia, prevalence is approximately 1 in 379,000 live births (1 in 190,000 male births) reported from a United States registry.

Of the primary immunodeficiencies, Common Variable Immunodeficiency is the most common after selective IgA deficiency and is noted to have a high prevalence of 1 in every 10000 to 50000 live births. CVID affects 1 in 25.000 individuals with some evidence of higher prevalence in those from northern Europe.

THI is usually diagnosed retrospectively, predominant in males with a male to female ratio of 2 to 1. The cause is unknown.
