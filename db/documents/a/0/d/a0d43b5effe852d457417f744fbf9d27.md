As stated above, the exact cause of obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD) is still a mystery.

By evaluating individuals who develop OCD after developing a brain lesion or stroke, we were able to localize OCD symptoms to certain areas of the brain.

There is likely dopaminergic and glutamatergic overactivity in frontostriatal pathways and diminished serotonergic and GABAergic neurotransmission in frontolimbic systems.
