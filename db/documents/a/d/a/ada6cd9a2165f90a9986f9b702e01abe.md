Purpura fulminans is best managed with an interprofessional team including infectious disease nurse practitioners. The treatment of all types of purpura fulminans starts with supportive care and adequate hydration.Â This is important because of the widespread thrombosis associated with this disease can lead to damage of multiple end organs. One should determine the primary cause and some patients may need blood products. If there is necrosis, surgical debridement has to be undertaken immediately.

The prognosis for these patients is guarded.
