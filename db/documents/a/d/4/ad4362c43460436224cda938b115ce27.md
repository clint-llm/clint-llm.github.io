ADPKD consists of several complications, including hypertension (most common, and develops at an earlier age), renal failure, cyst infection, severe abdominal and flank pain, and subarachnoid hemorrhage (most severe complication).

ARPKD has some complications of respiratory distress (common), portal hypertension, variceal bleeding, renal insufficiency.

JNPHP can be presented by hepatic and renal complications: Hepatic fibrosis, biliary duct enlargement, growth retardation, and renal failure.

MCKD includes hyperuricemia and gout.
