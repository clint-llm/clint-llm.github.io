Punctal stenosis refers to the narrowing or complete occlusion of the external opening of the lacrimal canaliculus. It may also be associated with occlusion of the common canalicular duct. This activity describes the treatment and evaluation of punctal stenosis and highlights the role of the healthcare team in managing patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology of punctal stenosis.
- Review the appropriate steps in the evaluation of punctal stenosis.
- Outline the management options for punctal stenosis.
- Describe the importance of collaboration and communication amongst the interprofessional team to enhance the delivery of care for patients affected by punctal stenosis.
