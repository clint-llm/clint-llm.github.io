- The symptoms of hypertensive encephalopathy are **insidious**. Headache, nausea, and vomiting gradually worsen with time and are followed by**non-localizing**neurologic symptoms. This is in contrast to the**abrupt**and**focal**neurologic symptoms found with ischemic stroke or intracerebral hemorrhage.

- CT is not sensitive enough to rule out brain edema but is essential in evaluating other causes of altered mental status.

- Avoid an overaggressive lowering of the blood pressure during the treatment of this condition as it may result in ischemic organ damage. The blood pressure should not be lowered any more than 25% of the baseline blood pressure within the first day of treatment.

- The most commonly used first-choice antihypertensive drug for this condition is nicardipine.
