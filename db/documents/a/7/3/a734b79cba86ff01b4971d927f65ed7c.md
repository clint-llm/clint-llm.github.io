The common history of primary sclerosing cholangitis is highly variable and unpredictable. A persistently low serum alkaline phosphatase level less than 1.5 times the upper limit of normal confers a good prognosis. small duct PSC have a good prognosis, with no reports of bile duct malignancy in this group, and progression to advanced liver disease is uncommon. The Mayo risk score, which includes age, serum bilirubin, serum albumin, serum AST, and history of variceal bleeding, is helpful in predicting survival in patients with PSC.
