Hypertension is the most prevalent non-communicable disease globally and it is a major risk factor for cardiovascular diseases, including coronary artery disease, cerebrovascular accidents, and peripheral arterial diseases.

Secondary hypertension shares these complications with essential hypertension, however, a few complications are much more common in secondary hypertension. People with secondary hypertension commonly present with resistant hypertension, defined as uncontrolled hypertension on maximally tolerated doses of at least three antihypertensive drugs including a diuretic.
