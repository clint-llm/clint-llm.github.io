The more commonly abused anxiolytic is the benzodiazepine class of medications. There are less data available for other drugs mentioned in this article; however, benzodiazepine misuse was most common amongst younger adults.  Adults aged 18 to 49 were accountable for the highest rate of misuse. Though adults aged 50 to 65 were most often prescribed benzodiazepines.

Risk factors associated with anxiolytic and sedative toxicity include

- White race

- Female sex

- Uninsured

- Unemployed

- Panic symptoms

- Other psychiatric symptoms

- Alcohol abuse or dependence

- Cigarette use

- Illicit drug use

- History of IV drug use
