Several mechanisms of injury have been theorized and include local pressure necrosis, corrosive damage from leakage of battery contents, heavy metal toxicity, and direct electrical discharge. Electrical injuries appear to be the most likely mechanism.

Disk batteries may cause serious problems if they become lodged in the nose, ears, or GI tract. The most common location resulting in serious sequelae is the esophagus. Batteries that traverse the esophagus often pass the GI tract successfully.
