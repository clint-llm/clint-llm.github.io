The etiology of anteroseptal myocardial infarction is like any other MI, but the culprit lesion usually involves the left anterior descending artery or a diagonal branch.

1. Obstructive: Thrombus or embolus obstructing the coronary blood flow.

2. Non-obstructive: This is also called myocardial infarction with non-obstructive coronary arteries (MINCOA) which includes atheromatous lesion, coronary spasm, myocardial bridging or coronary artery dissection, etc.
