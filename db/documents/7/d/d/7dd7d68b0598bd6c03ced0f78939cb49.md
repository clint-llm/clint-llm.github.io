Viruses are the leading cause of acute gastroenteritis both in the United States and globally, accounting for the majority of acute cases of community-acquired diarrhea for which care is sought and a diagnosis made. This activity describes the risk factors, evaluation, and management of viral gastroenteritis and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in enhancing care delivery for affected patients.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the most common viruses responsible for viral gastroenteritis. 
- Explain how to diagnose viral gastroenteritis. 
- Review management considerations for viral gastroenteritis. 
- Summarize the importance of improving care coordination, with particular emphasis on communication between interprofessional medical teams, to enhance prompt and thorough delivery of care to patients with viral gastroenteritis.
