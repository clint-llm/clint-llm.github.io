Approximately 2% of the world population is afflicted with psoriasis; in the United States and Canada, the prevalence is as high as 4%. In general, psoriasis has a bimodal peak of onset. Onset can peak at 20 to 30 years of age and 50 to 60 years of age. Guttate psoriasis accounts for less than 30% of all total cases of psoriasis.
