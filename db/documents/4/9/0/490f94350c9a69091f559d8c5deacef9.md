The most common etiologies of frontal bone fractures in adults are motor vehicle accidents (MVAs), falls, assaults, falling objects, and penetrating trauma.

In the pediatric community, MVAs were the most common etiology (25.7%), followed by sports-related injuries (16.1%), assault (14.7%), and falls (10.1%). When grouped by age range, the most common mechanism from 0 to 6.99 years old was falls (28.6%), compared with MVAs from 7 to 12.9 years old (31.9), and assaults from 13 to 18 years old.
