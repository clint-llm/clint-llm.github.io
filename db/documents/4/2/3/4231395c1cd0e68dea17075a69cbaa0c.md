Associated PTH resistance resulting in chronic hypocalcemia and hyperphosphatemia can lead to basal ganglia and subcortical white matter calcification and consequent neuropsychiatric disorders, movement disorders, and parkinsonism.

The prevalence of carpal tunnel syndrome is higher in patients with AHO.

Increased risk for sleep apnea has been reported in children with PHP1A.

Insulin sensitivity is reduced in patients with PHP1A, which increases the risk of impaired glucose tolerance in them.

Spinal stenosis is often described in patients with AHO, which can lead to lower extremity paraparesis.

Heterotopic ossifications in or around the joints may rarely result in limited joint movements.
