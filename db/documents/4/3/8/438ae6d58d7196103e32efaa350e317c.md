Weight loss surgery is an efficacious treatment for patients with obesity; however, patients undergoing these procedures should have a thorough preoperative evaluation to ensure the best outcomes via proper patient selection. This activity reviews the pre-operative assessment and preparation for bariatric surgery and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in improving care for patients who undergo obesity surgery. Bariatric surgery is a highly effective treatment for obesity that has shown not only to assist with weight loss but to improve metabolic comorbidities in this patient population. This activity reviews the indications and contraindications of bariatric surgery for patients with obesity. This activity evaluates the role of the an interprofessional team in the evaluation of comorbidities associated with obesity and their optimization before surgery.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the proper psychologic evaluation and counseling of the patient undergoing weight loss surgery.
- Identify the indications and contraindications for obesity surgery.
- Summarize the necessary testing for the pre-operative assessment and preparation for patients undergoing obesity surgery.
- Review the importance of improving care coordination among the interprofessional team to enhance the delivery of care for patients undergoing bariatric surgery.
