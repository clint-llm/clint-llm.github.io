The clinical features of rhabdomyolysis are very nonspecific, and the course depends on the underlying cause. Rhabdomyolysis has both local and systemic features, with early and late complications. The local features include muscle pain, weakness, bruising, swelling, and tenderness. Systemic features including fever, malaise, nausea, confusion, agitation, delirium, tea-colored urine, or anuria.
