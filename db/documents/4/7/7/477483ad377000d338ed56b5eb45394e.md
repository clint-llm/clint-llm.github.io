Prematurity, the size of the defect, degree of lung hypoplasia and pulmonary hypertension, and associated anomalies are some of the main factors that determine the morbidity and mortality associated with Congenital diaphragmatic hernia. With advances in ventilation strategies, management of pulmonary hypertension, and refinement of surgical techniques, the outcomes of infants with CDH have improved over the past few years. The reported overall survival rates in CDH range between 60% to 70%.
