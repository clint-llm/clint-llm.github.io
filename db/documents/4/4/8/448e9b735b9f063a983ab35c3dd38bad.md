Body dysmorphic disorder, or BDD, formally known as dysmorphophobia, is a psychiatric condition defined in the DSM 5 as a preoccupation with a perceived defect or flaw in oneâ€™s physical appearance that is either not noticeable or only slightly observable by others. This activity outlines the evaluation and treatment of body dysmorphic disorder and reviews the interprofessional team's role in managing patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology of body dysmorphic disorder.
- Explain the screening questions used to diagnose body dysmorphic disorder.
