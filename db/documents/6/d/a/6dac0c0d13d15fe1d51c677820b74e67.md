Luteinizing hormone is a gonadotropin hormone secreted by the pituitary and is essential for puberty and normal sexual function. Its deficiency results in sexual dysfunction and infertility. This activity outlines the evaluation and treatment of luteinizing hormone deficiency and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in evaluating and treating patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiology of luteinizing hormone deficiency.
- Review the steps in the evaluation of luteinizing hormone deficiency.
- Outline the management options available for luteinizing hormone deficiency.
- Describe some interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to patients with Luteinizing hormone deficiency and improve outcomes.
