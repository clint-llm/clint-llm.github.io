Exudative retinal detachment occurs when excess fluid accumulates in the subretinal space either due to hypersecretion of fluid into the subretinal space or defective transport of fluids from the subretinal space. Any inflammatory, idiopathic, infectious, surgical, neoplastic, vascular, or drug-induced etiology can lead to loss of integrity of the blood-retinal barrier, thereby leading to exudative retinal detachment.
