The mortality rate for Enterobacter infections is generally high. A study conducted by Kang et al. that analyzed 30-day mortality rates in Enterobacter bacteremia showed that of those receiving appropriate antibiotics, 24.6% died. In those with cephalosporin-resistant strains of Enterobacter, 34.7% died.Enterobacter infections in which pneumonia, UTI, and soft tissue infections were the primary clinical manifestation.
