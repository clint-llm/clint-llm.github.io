As above, anyone who develops an allergic reaction to wheat exposure should be checked for IgE specific to wheat via a skin prick test or serum IgE.

For those being worked up for WDEIA, SPT or specific IgE to wheat, gluten, and omega-5-gliadin should be ordered.

For patients being evaluated for baker's asthma, the lower limit with a positive predictive value of 100 percent was 2.32 kU/L for specific wheat IgE and 5.0 mm for wheat on SPT with wheat flour extracts.

For celiac disease, measurement of serum immunoglobulin A antibody to tissue transglutaminase (anti-tTG) should be the first screening test ordered.
