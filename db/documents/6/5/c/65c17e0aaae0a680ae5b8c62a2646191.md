The differential diagnosis for patients presenting with any combination of sudden onset of symptoms such as headache, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, or vertigo includes both central and peripheral disease processes.

Central causes:

- Acute demyelinating disorder such as multiple sclerosis (MS) or encephalitis

- Alcohol use disorder

- Cerebellar hemorrhage

- Cerebellar infarction

- Cerebellar neoplasm

- Cerebellitis

- Illicit drug use

- Medication toxicity (phenytoin or carbamazepine)

Peripheral causes:

- Benign paroxysmal positional vertigo (BPPV)

- Labyrinthitis

- Meniere disease

- Vestibular neuronitis
