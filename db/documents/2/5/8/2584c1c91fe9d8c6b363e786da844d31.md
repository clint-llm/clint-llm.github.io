Fluticasone is a medication used to manage and treat asthma, allergic rhinitis, emphysema, and atopic dermatitis. It is in the corticosteroid class of drugs. This activity outlines the indications, mechanism of action, adverse events, and contraindications for fluticasone as a valuable agent in the management of asthma, allergic rhinitis, emphysema, and atopic dermatitis (and other disorders when applicable) pertinent for members of the interprofessional team in the care of patients with asthma, allergic rhinitis, emphysema, atopic dermatitis, and related conditions.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the mechanism of action of fluticasone.
- Describe the potential adverse effects of fluticasone.
- Summarize appropriate monitoring for patients using fluticasone.
- Explain the importance of improving care coordination amongst the interprofessional team members to improve outcomes for patients receiving fluticasone.
