Benedikt syndrome is an eponymously named neurological condition secondary to specific damage in the midbrain that causes ipsilateral oculomotor nerve palsy, contralateral hemiparesis, and contralateral cerebellar ataxia and/or Holmes tremor and/or choreoathetosis. This activity reviews the evaluation and treatment of Benedikt syndrome and reviews the role of the interprofessional team in evaluating and treating patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the clinical findings in Benedikt syndrome.
- Outline the neurological structures that are damaged in Benedikt syndrome and the relationship to the clinical presentation.
- Review the differential diagnosis for Benedikt syndrome.
- Describe the importance of collaboration and communication among the interprofessional team to enhance care coordination for patients affected by Benedikt syndrome.
