- The co-administration of voclosporin with potent CYP3A4 inhibitors, for example, ketoconazole, itraconazole, and clarithromycin, are contraindicated.

- In patients with eGFR <45 mL/min/1.73 m2, using voclosporin is contraindicated unless benefits exceed risks.

- Voclosporin is not recommended in patients with hypertension with a baseline blood pressure of >165/105 mm Hg.

- Although there is not much data about the use of voclosporin in pregnant women and lactating women, voclosporin formulation contains alcohol, and the current recommendation is that voclosporin should be avoided in pregnant and lactating patients.
