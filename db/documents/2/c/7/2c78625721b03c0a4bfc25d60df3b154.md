**Disorders of Sleep**

It is now commonly accepted that sleep deprivation is a major cause of accidents, including traffic accidents.

The effect of drugs on sleep and sleep cycles has been another concern in the treatment of many disorders, including Parkinson, dementia, epilepsy, anxiety, and depression. There is quality of life and safety concerns in the management of sleep disorders.

**Hypnagogic Hypersynchrony**

In children, during drowsiness and early sleep, EEG may show very high amplitude slow waves or theta activity with occasional sharply contoured waveforms. This can mimic the 3 Hz spike-wave discharges seen in primary generalized epilepsies. It is important to be aware of the unique EEG characteristics of sleep in childhood to reduce the misleading diagnosis of epilepsy
