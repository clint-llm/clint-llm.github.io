Yaws, a treponemal disease caused by the spirochete Treponema pertenue, causes disease in 3 stages with the last being most specific and manifesting as destructive osteitis. Antibiotic resistance is a concern with yaws and optimizing antibiotic stewardship is important to prevent worsening of this problem. This activity reviews the evaluation and management of yaws and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in the care of affected patients.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the common presentation of a patient with yaws. 
- Identify treatment considerations for patients with yaws.
- Explain why antibiotic resistance is a concern with yaws.
- Explain the importance of improving coordination amongst the interprofessional team to enhance care for patients affected by yaws.
