The human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) is an enveloped retrovirus that contains 2 copies of a single-stranded RNA genome. It causes the acquired immunodeficiency syndrome (AIDS) that is the last stage of HIV disease. This activity explains when this condition should be considered on a differential diagnosis, articulates how to properly evaluate for this condition, and highlights the interprofessional team's role in caring for patients with HIV.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the signs and symptoms of HIV disease.
- Explain how the diagnosis of HIV disease is confirmed.
- Explain treatment considerations for patients with HIV disease.
