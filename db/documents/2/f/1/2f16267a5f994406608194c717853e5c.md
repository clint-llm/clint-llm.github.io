Alfentanil is a short-acting opioid analgesic commonly compared to fentanyl, a ubiquitous medication in the hospital setting, along with remifentanil and sufentanil. It has FDA approval as an anesthetic agent in adults and children undergoing general anesthesia and monitored anesthesia care. This activity reviews the indications, target patient, administration, adverse effects, mechanism of action, and monitoring of alfentanil as part of an interprofessional strategy in pain management.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the target patient population and appropriate scenario for alfentanil use.
- Describe the mechanism of action of alfentanil.
- Review the appropriate monitoring of alfentanil.
- Outline interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication in the use of alfentanil to improve outcomes in pain management where its use is indicated.
