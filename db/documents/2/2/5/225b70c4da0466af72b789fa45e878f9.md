Because of various economic and social factors, heroin is one of the most commonly abused opioids in the world today. In 2012, annual prevalence rates for heroin use without a needle were 0.3% for 8th-grade students and 0.4 percent for 10th and 12th-grade students. For all grades, the annual prevalence of use of heroin with a needle was 0.4%. Heroin-related emergency department visits had increased from 33,900 in 1990  to 213,118 in 2009. Heroin-related overdose deaths increased from 2300 in 1991 to 15.958 in 2017. There was a 7 fold increase in the total number of heroin overdose deaths in this period.
