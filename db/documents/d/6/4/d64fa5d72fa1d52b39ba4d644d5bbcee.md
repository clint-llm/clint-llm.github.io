The arteries of the lower limb are particularly susceptible to atherosclerotic plaque buildup and peripheral arterial disease where the arterial lumen of the lower extremities becomes progressively occluded by atherosclerotic plaque, which can lead to decreased arterial blood flow.

Another surgical intervention involving the posterior tibial artery involves posterior tibial arterial aneurysm repair. Aneurysms of the infrapopliteal region are extremely rare, and most of them are reportedly associated with either infection or inflammatory processes.
