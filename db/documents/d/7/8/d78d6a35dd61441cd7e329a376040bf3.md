Hip fractures are fractures of the proximal femur and are commonly seen in the elderly population following a fall. This type of injury has a high rate of morbidity and mortality. To reduce this, surgical intervention is often required. This activity reviews the assessment and management of hip fractures and highlights the importance of the interprofessional team.

**Objectives:**
- Summarize the etiology of hip fractures.
- Outline the key aspects of the evaluation in hip fractures.
- Review the management options available for hip fractures.
- Explain some interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance hip fractures and improve outcomes.
