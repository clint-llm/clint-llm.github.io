Malignant atrophic papulosis is a rare vasculopathy affecting the skin, gastrointestinal tract, and central nervous system. The systemic form has an aggressive clinical course with a high mortality rate. This activity illustrates the evaluation and management of malignant atrophic papulosis and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in improving care for patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the physical exam findings associated with malignant atrophic papulosis. 
- Review the risk factors for developing malignant atrophic papulosis. 
- Explain the common complications of malignant atrophic papulosis. 
- Summarize the importance of improving care coordination among interprofessional team members to improve outcomes for patients affected by malignant atrophic papulosis.
