All healthcare providers should be competent with the methods to perform abdominal palpation of a gravid uterus and the significance of the findings. The findings observed should be documented and can be used to guide further obstetric management. Identifying pregnancies complicated by malpresentation and referral to appropriate facilities may lead to improved outcomes for both neonate and mother.

Offering an external cephalic version (ECV) for breech presentation can contribute to the safe lowering of the primary cesarean delivery rate.
