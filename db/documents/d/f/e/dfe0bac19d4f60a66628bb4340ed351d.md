Bethanechol is available in oral and subcutaneous preparations. Tablets are available in 5 mg, 10 mg, 25 mg, and 50 mg dosages.

**Postoperative Urinary Retention**

- 10 to 50 mg three or four times daily.

**Postpartum Nonobstructive Urinary Retention**

- 10 to 50 mg three or four times daily.

**Neurogenic Bladder**

- 10 to 50 mg three or four times daily.

The dosage for bethanechol is decided on a case-by-case basis, which is dependent on the type and severity of the urinary retention. Patients are initiated on a 5 to 10 mg dosage to ascertain the minimum effective dosage and are repeated in hourly intervals until an adequate response transpires. Patients should not consume greater than 50 mg per single dose as adverse effects may occur. The agent is preferred to be administered on an empty stomach as nausea and vomiting can incite.
