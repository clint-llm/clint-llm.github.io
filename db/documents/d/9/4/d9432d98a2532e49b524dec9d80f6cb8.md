Anthropometry, or the measurement of body parameters, helps in the diagnosis of malnutrition and obesity. Height assessment is essential from a clinical, anthropological, and forensic standpoint. It is an absolutely critical part of the well-child visit and can aid in diagnosing multiple conditions. This activity describes the significance associated with height assessment, the different methods that can be used for its assessment across age groups, and the methods employed for skeletal remains in forensic science. These methods often require a deeper understanding of the subject, best known to trained nursing staff and clinicians. It is an essential skill that requires consistent methods of practice to ensure reliable measurements are obtained.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the importance and associated indications of height assessment.
- Describe the equipment, personnel, preparation, and technique recommended for height assessment.
- Review the potential complications in performing height assessment.
- Explain the clinical significance of performing height assessments.
