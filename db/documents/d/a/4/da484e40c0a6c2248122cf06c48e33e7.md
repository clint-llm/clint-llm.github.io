- Gastric perforation is rare today because of the availability of PPIs.

- Iatrogenic and trauma account for most gastric perforations today.

- The patient will present with signs of an acute abdomen.

- Free air will be visualized on plain x-rays or CT scans.

- Surgery is the only viable treatment for gastric perforation.

- When treated promptly, the prognosis is improved, but the risk of complications is high.
