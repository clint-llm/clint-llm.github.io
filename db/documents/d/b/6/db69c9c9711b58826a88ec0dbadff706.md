Clinical and/or radiologic detection of a fracture of any portion of the talus warrants consultation with an orthopedic surgeon for optimal management. An orthopedic specialist should guide nonoperative, emergent primary surgical, and definitive surgical management of these fractures. In the event of persistent clinical suspicion for radiographically-occult talar fracture, CT is recommended, and consultation with a radiologist may be helpful for optimal study acquisition.
