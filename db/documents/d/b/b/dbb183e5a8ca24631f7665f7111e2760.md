Renal cysts are clinically insignificant or may cause end-stage renal failure and develops due to genetic or non-genetic causes in children and adults. Cystic kidney diseases can be part of multisystemic disorders with extrarenal symptoms. The most common cystic kidney disease in adults is autosomal dominant polycystic kidney disease. The cystic kidney is a disease diagnosed by renal size and cyst localization, as well as extrarenal symptoms.
