In 2017, it is estimated that there will about 252,000 new cases of invasive breast cancer diagnosed in the United States along with 63,000 of noninvasive breast cancer (in-situ) cases. About 40,000 women are expected to die, although mortality rates have been declining. In the United States, breast cancer is the second most common cause of cancer-related deaths after lung cancer. Mortality rates are however higher in African Americans compared to whites.  The risk of developing and dying from breast cancer is lower in Asians, Hispanics, and Native Americans.
