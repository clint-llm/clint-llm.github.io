The management of insomnia is with an interprofessional team consisting of a nurse practitioner, primary care provider, neurologist, psychiatrist, and an internist. Insomnia is one of the significant health concerns impairing the quality of life of the one-third population of the United States. Insomnia also poses a substantial economic burden on the society by affecting the workplace productivity with an estimate ofÂ greater than $100 billion health care cost burden. Insomnia is considered a risk factor for cardiovascular diseases, diabetes, obesity, hypertension, and asthma. Cognitive behavioral therapy is the mainstay in the management of insomnia disorder. However, lack of awareness and limited financial support restricts the users from its benefits. The outcome of patients with insomnia is poor. Most individuals continue to have difficulty sleeping and consequently lead a poor quality of life.
