Vestibular schwannomas are benign tumors involving the vestibular portion of the vestibulocochlear nerve (CN VIII). The most common location is an inferior vestibular nerve. They are well-circumscribed encapsulated tumors arising from the perineural elements of the Schwann cells. They usually cause splaying and displacement of the nerve fibers rather than engagement.
