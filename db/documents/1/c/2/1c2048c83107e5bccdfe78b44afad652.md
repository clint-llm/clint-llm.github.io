Differential diagnosis of breast milk jaundice include:

- Physiological jaundice of newborns

- Hemolysis associated hyperbilirubinemia ( ABO, G6PD, hereditary spherocytosis, others)

- Breastfeeding jaundice ( inadequate milk intake and dehydration)

- Genetic causes ( Crigler-Najjar, pyruvate kinase deficiency, and Gilbert syndromes)

- Cholestasis (Biliary atresia, neonatal hepatitis, choledochal cyst, and disorders of bilirubin excretion)
