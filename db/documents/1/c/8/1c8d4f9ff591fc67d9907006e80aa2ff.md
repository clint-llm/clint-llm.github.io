Autoimmune pancreatitis is a clinical condition characterized by inflammation of the pancreas from an autoimmune etiology. It is an uncommon cause of pancreatitis but can present with significant morbidity and mortality because of repeated episodes of acute pancreatitis. It can be associated with other autoimmune conditions, and timely diagnosis and management are important to achieve good patient outcomes. This activity reviews the evaluation and management of autoimmune pancreatitis and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in the care of patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the pathophysiology of autoimmune pancreatitis as an autoimmune process and associations of the entity with other diseases.
- Identify the clinical presentation of autoimmune pancreatitis.
- Review the management of autoimmune pancreatitis including corticosteroids and the choice of immunomodulators in managing the condition. 
- Outline the importance of collaboration and coordination among the interprofessional team to improve recognition and treatment of patients with autoimmune pancreatitis.
