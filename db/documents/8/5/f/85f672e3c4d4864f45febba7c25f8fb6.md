The term TORCH complex or TORCHes infection includes toxoplasmosis, others (syphilis, hepatitis B), rubella, cytomegalovirus, herpes simplex. These are caused by toxoplasma gondii, treponema pallidum, hepatitis B virus, rubella virus, cytomegalovirus, and herpes simplex virus (HSV), respectively. Human immunodeficiency virus and Zika virus are sometimes included in this grouping. This activity highlights the role of interprofessional team in evaluation and management of patients with TORCH complex.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the etiologic agents of TORCH infections.
- Describe the clinical manifestations of the TORCH complex in neonates.
- Summarize the management of congenital infections.
