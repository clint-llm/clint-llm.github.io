Patients with toxic and nutritional optic neuropathies typically present with slow and insidious bilateral and very symmetric visual loss. The exception to this is methanol-related optic neuropathy, where the visual loss follows methanol injection. Patients may report decreased vision, visual field defects, difficulty seeing in low-light conditions, and decreased visualization of colors.
