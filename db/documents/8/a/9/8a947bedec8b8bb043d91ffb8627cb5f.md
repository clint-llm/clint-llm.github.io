Trench fever is caused by a fastidious aerobic gram-negative rod known as Bartonella quintana.Pediculus humanus corporis (body louse), Pediculus humans capitis (head louse), and Pediculus humans pubis (pubic louse), with the body louse being the most common vector. There has been at least one report about the possibility of transmission by bed bugs (Cimex lectularius) and cat fleas.Bartonella quintana bacteria.
