Obesity is a worldwide pandemic, resulting in comorbidities, socioeconomic costs, and reduced quality of life. Severe obesity, those with a BMI over 40 kg/m^2, is an indication for a patient to undergo bariatric surgery. Main surgical procedures performed have elements of restriction, malabsorption, or a combination of both mechanisms. This activity reviews the evaluation and surgical treatment of severe obesity and highlights the role of the interprofessional team in evaluating and treating this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Identify the requirements for a patient to undergo bariatric surgery.
- Review the bariatric surgeries indicated for severe obesity endorsed by the American Society of Metabolic and Bariatric Surgeons.
- Summarize the risks and benefits of bariatric surgery. 
- Describe the extensive preoperative assessment by the interprofessional team that a patient must undergo before bariatric surgery.
