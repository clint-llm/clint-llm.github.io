Geographic tongue is usually diagnosed by its characteristic history and clinical features. In cases of atypical lesions, differential diagnosis of geographic tongue includes erythroplakia, lichen planus, candidiasis, contact stomatitis, leukoplakia, trauma, and aphthous ulcer.

- Lichen planus

- Candidiasis

- Contact stomatitis

- Leukoplakia

- Trauma

- Aphthous ulcer

- Squamous cell cancer

- Plaque psoriasis
