Patterns of spread for bacterial conjunctivitis include hand to eye, eye contact with fomite, and person to person through respiratory droplets.Haemophilus influenzae, followed by Streptococcus pneumoniae and Moraxella catarrhalis.Haemophilus influenzae and Streptococcus pneumoniae responsible for a smaller percentage of cases.Staphylococcus aureus is more commonly found in adults and the elderly but is also present in pediatric cases of bacterial conjunctivitis.Staphylococcus aureus (MRSA).Pseudomonas aeruginosa is more likely to be the isolate from critically ill, hospitalized patients.Neisseria gonorrhoeae and Chlamydia trachomatis resulting in acute bacterial conjunctivitis.
