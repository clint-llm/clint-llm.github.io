Nail biopsies are performed in order to diagnose and treat a large variety of pathologies, some of which are potentially fatal. The nail biopsy is not simply one technique but a variety of techniques, each of which plays a specific role in diagnoses. This activity outlines proper indications, contraindications, preparations, and techniques for nail biopsies and highlights the healthcare team's role in evaluating and treating nail pathologies.

**Objectives:**
- Describe the relevant anatomy of the nail unit pertinent to nail biopsies.
- Identify pathologies that require a nail biopsy.
- Outline the steps in performing each of the nail biopsy techniques.
- Review how interprofessional team collaboration can improve patient outcomes when the case warrants a nail biopsy.
