Erythroderma is a clinical finding overlying one of a myriad of potential triggers, including dermatoses, drugs, malignancies, and idiopathic causes.

- Acanthosis nigricans

- Acute complications of sarcoidosis

- Cutaneous T-cell lymphoma

- Dermatologic manifestations of graft versus host disease

- Allergic contact dermatitis

- Bullous pemphigoid

- Familial benign pemphigus (Hailey-Hailey disease)

- Irritant contact dermatitis

- Lichen planus

- Pityriasis rubra pilaris

- Plaque psoriasis

- Reactive arthritis

- Pediatric atopic dermatitis

- Pemphigus foliaceus

- Seborrheic dermatitis

- Stasis dermatitis
