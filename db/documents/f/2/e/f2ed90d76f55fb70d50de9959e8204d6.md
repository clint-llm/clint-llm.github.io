Indications for fogging include the following:

- To control accommodation during retinoscopy.

- Refraction of pediatric patients who have excessive accommodation.

- Refraction of patients with asthenopic symptoms, which tend to be more significantly prevalent in hyperopes.

- When the clinician has a reasonable suspicion of a patient with an over-minus prescribed lens correction.

- Refraction of a patient with latent hyperopia (such patients will report the same visual acuity over a range of powers).

- in certain cases of ocular deviation and amblyopia.
