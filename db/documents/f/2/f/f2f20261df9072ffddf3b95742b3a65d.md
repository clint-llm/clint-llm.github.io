The diagnosis and management of AP window require an interprofessional team that includes a pediatrician, cardiologist, cardiac surgeon, intensivist, and radiologist. The disorder is typically managed with surgery, but the key is that the child does not have any other associated congenital heart defects. Once the surgery is performed, the infant is usually monitored by cardiac or critical care nurses. If this is an isolated defect, the outcomes are good and are typically good, even when associated with other congenital heart defects. Pharmacists should review the dosing of medications and provide teaching to families of patients.
