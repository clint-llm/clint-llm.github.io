CON can be produced by an extrinsic or intrinsic lesion, causing a mass effect anywhere along the optic nerve.

- Infectious

- Aspergilloma

- Inflammatory

- Idiopathic orbital inflammation-pseudotumor
- Sarcoidosis
- Thyroid orbitopathy-Graves disease
- IgG4 pachymeningitis

- Vascular

- Aneurysm
- Carotid-cavernous fistula
- Lymphangioma
- Orbital varix
- Ectatic internal carotid artery

- Traumatic

- Hematoma
- Fracture

- Neoplastic

- Hemangioma
- Schwannoma
- Meningioma
- Glioma (Neurofibromatosis Type 1)
- Lymphoma
- Sarcoma
- Metastatic
- Pituitary adenoma
- Craniopharyngioma
- Dermoid

- Bone tumors/lesion

- Fibrous dysplasia
- Paget's disease
- Osteoma
- Osteopertrosis
- Hyperostosis
- Langerhans cell histiocytosis

- Other

- Mucocele
- Granulomatous meningitis
