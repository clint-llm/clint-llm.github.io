Angina is the most common symptom of ischemic heart disease, which is the major cause of morbidity and mortality worldwide. Approximately 9 million patients in the USA have symptoms of angina, though its treatment is challenging. This activity describes the evaluation and management of angina and reviews the role of the interprofessional team in improving care for patients with this condition.

**Objectives:**
- Outline the pathophysiology of angina.
- Review the evaluation of angina.
- Summarize the treatment and management options available for angina.
- Describe some interprofessional team strategies for improving care coordination and communication to advance treatment for angina and improve outcomes.
