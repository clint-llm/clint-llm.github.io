Digoxin toxicity manifests itself in acute and chronic forms. Acute toxicity is mainly the result of dosage errors, suicidal ingestions, and accidental ingestion of CAS containing plants. Chronic toxicity is usually due to drug-drug interactions, conditions that alter the protein binding resulting in increased free state CAS, changes in gut absorption of the drug, and impaired renal clearance of the drug.
