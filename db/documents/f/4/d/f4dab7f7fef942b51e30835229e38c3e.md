ACEP Guidelines for managing hypertension in the ED

- In asymptomatic patients with elevated BP, routine screening for end-organ damage is not recommended.

- In select patients (poor follow-up) screening for elevated creatinine may identify the renal injury.

- In asymptomatic patients with elevated BP, immediate medical intervention is not necessary.

- Selected patients with poor follow-up can be treated in the ED for elevated blood pressure.

- All patients with asymptomatic elevated BP should be referred to a cardiology outpatient clinic.
